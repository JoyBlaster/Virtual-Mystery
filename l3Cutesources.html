Citing your sources correctly

Every time you quote or paraphrase, you must include an in-text or footnote citation clearly identifying the original author. Each citation must correspond to a full reference in the reference list or bibliography at the end of your paper.


This acknowledges the source of your information, avoiding plagiarism, and it helps your readers locate the source for themselves if they would like to learn more.
There are many different citation styles, each with its own rules. A few common styles are APA, MLA, and Chicago. Your instructor may assign a particular style for you to use, or you may be able to choose. The most important thing is to apply one style consistently throughout the text.


Citations styles

The credits will show 2 styles of citations, APA and Harvard style of citation but we will only focus on APA for now

APA Reference style


What to do

Year of publication

In round brackets followed by a full stop e.g (2016)

Article titles

Not italicised nor enclosed in quotations marks; only the first letter is capitalised e.g Effects of quitting smoking

Book titles

Capitalise only the first letter of the first word(unless the word is a proper noun); italicise book titles e.g Service Marketing

Book chapters

Page numbers for book chapters are given immediately after book title in round brackets
e.g Cross-cultural education(pp.75-105)

Journal titles

Italicise journal titles including the volume number; every first letter of major words in the journal title is capitalised
e.g Health Psychology

Internet sources

For online sources with doi* , it should be indicated as URL
e.g http://doi.org/10.1080.1422200410001676305
*DOI is short for digital object identifier. All legitimate items published online have DOI. Use this instead of URL if provided.


What not to do

No retrieval date

Does not include retrieval date and "Retrieved from" for online sources. URLs should be embedded in the reference

No database name require

Not necessary to include the name of the database when referemcing online journals or eBook collections

No publisher location

Publisher location is no longer included in the reference

In conclusion

So when researching for a thesis or an important research quesiton, remember when citing sources to cite properly using one of the many citation styles.You may have learnt APA for now but there are still alot more to learn out there.
